He received full higher education on a specialty &#8220;Optical and opto-electronic systems&#8221; and got a qualification of an engineer-optician-researcher.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From 1985 till 1986 he worked as an engineer at the Research and Development Department of the Ivan Franko State University of Lviv.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From 1986 till 1987 he was an engineer of the Department of Experimental Physics.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From 1987 till 1990 he was a post-graduate student on the Department of Experimental Physics of Ivan Franko State University of Lviv.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">In 1990 Vasyl Stadnyk defended his Ph.D. thesis named &#8220;<em>Electron polarization of </em><em>А</em><em><sub>2</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>4</sub></em> <em>group crystals with incommensurate phases</em>&#8220;. He had been awarded a candidate degree of sciences in physics and mathematics, specialty – solid state physics.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From 1990 till 1991 he worked as a junior researcher and from 1991 till 1992 as a senior researcher at the Research and Development Department of Ivan Franko State University of Lviv.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From1992 til 1999 Vasyl Stadnyk worked as an assistant, during next 1999–2007 years – a docent of the Department of Experimental Physics of Ivan Franko State University of Lviv (since October 11, 1999, Ivan Franko National University of Lviv).</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">In 1999 he was awarded the academic status of an associate professor of the Department of Experimental Physics.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">In 2006 he defended his thesis for a doctor degree named &#8220;<em>Baric changes of the refractive parameters of dielectric crystals</em>&#8220;. He was awarded the scientific degree of a doctor of  sciences in physics and mathematics, specialty –  physics of semiconductors and insulators.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">From 2007 till 2008 Vasyl Stadnyk worked as a docent, from 2008 till 2016 – a professor of the Department of Solid State Physics of Ivan Franko National University of Lviv.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">In 2011 he was awarded the the academic status of a professor of the Department of Solid State Physics.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">Since May 2016 he is a chairperson of the Department of General Physics of the Ivan Franko National University of Lviv.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">Currently Vasyl Stadnyk is a scientific supervisor of the state budget theme FZ-53F named &#8220;<em>New materials for functional electronics on the basis of semiconductor and dielectric crystals of </em><em>А</em><em><sub>4</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>6</sub></em><em> and</em> <em>А</em><em><sub>2</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>4</sub></em> <em>groups</em>&#8221; and the economic contractual theme F76/77-2017 named <em>&#8220;Synthesis of a new class of crystalline materials of А<sub>2</sub>ВХ<sub>4</sub> group and study of the prospects of their effective application in electromagnetic radiation controlling devices&#8221;</em>. He is an author of 280 scientific publications.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">Vasyl Stadnyk was a supervisor of 4 defended thesis. Currently he supervises the research work of 2 postgraduate students of the Department of General Physics, 3<sup>rd</sup> year of study.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify"><span style="color: #000000">Teaching and methodological work of Vasyl Stadnyk includes 2 monographs and 3 tutorials.</span></p></div></section><section class='section'><h2>Projects</h2><div><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">Professor <strong>Vasyl Stadnyk</strong> is a scientific supervisor of the state budget theme FZ-53F named &#8220;<em>New materials for functional electronics on the basis of semiconductor and dielectric crystals of </em><em>А</em><em><sub>4</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>6</sub></em><em> and</em> <em>А</em><em><sub>2</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>4</sub></em> <em>groups</em>&#8221; (01.01.2017 – 31.12.2019) and the economic contractual theme F76/77-2017  named <em>&#8220;Synthesis of a new class of crystalline materials of А<sub>2</sub>ВХ<sub>4</sub> group and study of the prospects of their effective application in electromagnetic radiation controlling devices&#8221;</em> (30.08.2017 – 31.12.2017).</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #000000; text-decoration: underline;">The state budget theme &#8220;<em>New materials for functional electronics on the basis of semiconductor and dielectric crystals of </em><em>А</em><em><sub>4</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>6</sub></em><em> and</em> <em>А</em><em><sub>2</sub></em><em>ВХ</em><em><sub>4</sub></em> <em>groups&#8221;:</em></span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;"><em>Aim of the work:</em> to develop the technological fundamentals of synthesis of nanostructured materials based on А<sub>2</sub>ВХ<sub>4</sub> and А<sub>2</sub>ВХ<sub>6 </sub>crystals, including those having birefringence sign inversion and high anisotropy of physical characteristics; to study unit cell parameters, space group symmetry, transmission, reflection and absorption spectra; temperature and spectral changes in refractive indices, birefringences, optical activity and dichroism.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">The work is supposed to investigate the structural transformations, the impact of the structural elements replacement on the optical properties as well as the impact of external factors (pressure, temperature, electric and magnetic fields, radiation) on the changing of the electronic properties of bulk crystals and nanocrystals.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">The crystals of LiNH<sub>4</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> (LAS) in α- and β-modification, Rb<sub>2</sub>ZnCl<sub>4</sub> (RTCZ) crystals with incommensurate phase, as well as In<sub>x</sub>Tl<sub>1–х</sub>І substitutional solid solutions were synthesized. The influence of uniaxial stresses on the spectral and temperature changes of the refractive indices <em>n<sub>i</sub></em> of RTCZ crystals was investigated. It has been established that in the studied spectral and temperature ranges, the dispersion <em>n<sub>i</sub></em>(λ) of mechanically free and stressed crystals is normal. The baric changes of <em>n<sub>i</sub></em> have been studied and crystal optics parameters of mechanically deformed crystals calculated. The band-energy structure of LAS crystals is calculated. As the quantitative measure of the dispersion of electronic levels the effective mass of charge carriers obtained for the top of the valence band and the bottom of the conduction band was considered. Using the calculated full and partial densities of states, the genesis of the calculated bands is established.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">7 papers in journals with impact factor, 6 abstracts in the materials of the international conferences indexed in Scopus, and 1 patent of Ukraine for useful model were published, as well as  the  thesis   by M. V. Kovalenko entitled “<em>Electronic energy structure, opto-spectral and sensory properties of nanostructures based on ZnO</em>” (a supervisor was a docent O. V. Bovhyra, specialty 01.04.10 – physics of semiconductors and insulators) defended.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #000000; text-decoration: underline;">The economic contractual theme &#8220;Synthesis of a new class of crystalline materials of А<sub>2</sub>ВХ<sub>4</sub> group and study of the prospects of their effective application in electromagnetic radiation controlling devices&#8221;:</span></span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">The crystals of LiNH<sub>4</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> (LAS), K<sub>1.75</sub>(NH<sub>4</sub>)<sub>0.25</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> (PAS) and (NH<sub>4</sub>)<sub>2</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> (AS) of good optical quality were synthesized, their spatial symmetry groups and parameters of elementary cells were determined; dispersion and temperature dependences of refractive indices <em>n<sub>і</sub></em>(λ) and birefringence Δ<em>n<sub>і</sub></em>(λ) of these crystals were studied; for PAS crystal at a wavelength λ=500 nm at temperatures <em>T</em><sub>01</sub>≈541 K and <em>T</em><sub>02</sub>≈589 K two isotropic points are found which correspond to the equality of there fractive indices <em>n<sub>z</sub></em>=<em>n<sub>y</sub> </em>and <em>n<sub>z</sub></em>=<em>n<sub>x</sub></em>; the temperature-spectral-baric diagrams of the isotropic state of the K<sub>1.75</sub>(NH<sub>4</sub>)<sub>0.25</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> crystal, which allow to find the correlation between the deformations of the optical indicatrix due to temperature changes, uniaxial compression, or wavelength change have been constructed; It was proposed to use LiNH<sub>4</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> and K<sub>1,75</sub>(NH<sub>4</sub>)<sub>0,25</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> crystals as new crystals with anisotropic point in accessible temperature and spectral ranges.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;">Based on the results, 2 articles were published in journals with impact factor and one article is prepared for publication; 2 abstracts in the materials of international conferences indexed by Scopus are published.</span></p></div></section><section class='section'><h2>Teaching materials</h2><div><p><strong><em>Monographs:</em></strong></p><ol><li style="text-align: justify;"><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Стадник В.
